### What indoor air quality solutions are

Indoor air quality solutions are a product category within HVAC that addresses airborne contaminants, excess moisture, and inadequate ventilation inside enclosed spaces. The category includes air filtration systems rated from MERV 8 to MERV 16, ventilation equipment that controls air exchange rates, and air purification devices that reduce pollutant concentrations. [A licensed air conditioning contractor](doc:air-conditioning-contractor) selects and installs the correct combination of these components based on the building's size, occupancy, and existing HVAC layout. Indoor air quality solutions are a sub-type of HVAC products, placing them within the same service and maintenance ecosystem as cooling and heating equipment. The alternate names for this category include IAQ solutions and home air quality systems, terms used interchangeably across product specifications and service agreements.

### Measurable attributes of indoor air quality solutions

Indoor air quality solutions carry three primary measurable attributes that determine performance. The first is particle filtration efficiency, expressed as a MERV rating between 8 and 16. A MERV 8 filter captures large particles such as dust and pollen. A MERV 16 filter captures fine particulates including mold spores and bacteria-carrying droplets. The second attribute is air exchange rate, measured in air changes per hour (ACH). A higher ACH value means stale indoor air is replaced with filtered air more frequently. The third attribute is pollutant reduction capacity, expressed as a percentage of target contaminants removed per cycle. [Energy efficiency](doc:energy-efficiency) is directly affected by filter MERV rating, because denser filters increase static pressure and force the air handler to work harder. Selecting the correct MERV rating for a given system balances filtration performance against airflow resistance.

### How indoor air quality solutions relate to the broader HVAC system

Indoor air quality solutions are a component category within the broader HVAC product family, not a standalone system. Air filtration systems mount inside the air handler or ductwork and treat air on every pass through the system. Ventilation equipment introduces controlled amounts of outdoor air to dilute indoor pollutants. Air purification devices attach to supply or return plenums and treat air before distribution. A [whole-home dehumidifier](doc:dehumidifier) is the primary upstream component that removes excess moisture before it reaches filtration and purification stages, making it the foundational IAQ device in humid climates. ### Common problems that indoor air quality solutions address

Indoor air quality solutions target four categories of problems found in residential and commercial buildings.

- **Particulate contamination**: Particulate contamination includes dust, pollen, pet dander, and mold spores circulating through ductwork. A MERV 8 to MERV 16 rated filter captures these particles before they recirculate into occupied spaces.

- **Excess humidity**: Excess humidity above 60% relative humidity promotes mold growth on surfaces and inside duct systems. Whole-home dehumidification equipment reduces moisture to the 45% to 55% range recommended for healthy indoor environments.

- **Volatile organic compounds (VOCs)**: VOCs off-gas from building materials, cleaning products, and furnishings. Air purification devices using activated carbon or photocatalytic oxidation reduce VOC concentrations in the breathing zone.

- **Inadequate air exchange**: Inadequate air exchange allows carbon dioxide and odors to accumulate when buildings are tightly sealed. Ventilation equipment set to the correct ACH value dilutes these contaminants with filtered outdoor air.

### Where indoor air quality solutions apply

Indoor air quality solutions apply in residential buildings and commercial buildings where occupants spend extended time in enclosed spaces. Residential applications include single-family homes, condominiums, and multi-family units where occupant density and building tightness determine the required ACH and MERV specifications. Commercial applications include offices, retail spaces, and light industrial facilities where higher occupant loads and chemical off-gassing demand higher pollutant reduction capacity.
