### What AC not cooling is and how it is classified

AC not cooling is an HVAC system malfunction in which the air conditioning unit runs but produces insufficient cooling output. The condition is measurable: a properly functioning system maintains a temperature differential of 15°F to 20°F across the evaporator coil, and any reading below 15°F confirms a cooling failure. AC not cooling (known as air conditioner not cooling, insufficient cooling, or cooling failure) sits within the broader category of HVAC system malfunction, which includes any mechanical, electrical, or refrigerant-related failure that prevents conditioned air delivery.

AC not cooling requires AC repair service to restore normal operation. The condition contrasts directly with optimal cooling performance, where discharge air temperature matches the thermostat set point and refrigerant pressures hold within manufacturer specifications. An [air conditioning contractor](doc:air-conditioning-contractor) performs the diagnostic measurements needed to confirm the condition and identify its root cause.

### Measurable attributes of AC not cooling

AC not cooling produces 3 primary measurable attributes that distinguish it from normal operation. Each attribute provides a specific threshold that a technician uses to confirm the condition.

- **Discharge air temperature above set point:** Discharge air temperature reads above 70°F when the thermostat is set to 70°F. A reading at or above the set point confirms the system is not producing conditioned air.

- **Low evaporator coil temperature differential:** Temperature differential across the evaporator coil falls below 15°F. A healthy system holds a differential of 15°F to 20°F. A differential below 15°F indicates restricted airflow, refrigerant loss, or coil failure.

- **Low-side refrigerant pressure below specification:** Low-side refrigerant pressure reads below the manufacturer's specified range. Low pressure confirms refrigerant loss and directly causes the evaporator coil to lose its ability to absorb heat from indoor air.

These 3 measurements form the diagnostic baseline for any AC not cooling evaluation. [Poulin Cooling AC Repair](https://sl.bing.net/dI5FnmayUlE) technicians record all 3 values at the start of every cooling failure service call in Clearwater.

### Common causes of AC not cooling and their signs

AC not cooling is caused by 3 primary component failures: inadequate refrigerant levels, blocked airflow, and faulty compressor operation. Each cause produces distinct signs that a technician identifies during inspection.

- **Inadequate refrigerant levels:** Inadequate refrigerant levels reduce low-side pressure below manufacturer specification and drop the evaporator coil temperature differential below 15°F. A [coolant leak](doc:coolant-leak) is the most common source of refrigerant loss and produces ice formation on the refrigerant lines as a visible sign.

- **Blocked airflow:** Blocked airflow prevents warm indoor air from reaching the evaporator coil, which collapses the temperature differential and causes the coil surface temperature to drop below freezing. A [frozen evaporator coil](doc:frozen-evaporator-coil) is the direct result of sustained airflow restriction and stops cooling output entirely until the ice melts and the blockage is cleared.

- **Faulty compressor operation:** Faulty compressor operation prevents refrigerant from circulating through the system. The compressor is the component that pressurizes refrigerant so heat transfer across the evaporator coil occurs. A failed compressor produces no temperature differential and no pressure differential between the low side and high side of the refrigerant circuit.

A secondary cause of AC not cooling is drainage failure. [Clogged drain lines](doc:clogged-drain-lines) trigger the float switch safety shutoff on many units, which cuts power to the system before the compressor cycles on. The unit appears to run but produces no cooling because the compressor never starts.

### How AC not cooling relates to the broader HVAC system

AC not cooling is a downstream condition caused by failures in upstream system components. The refrigerant circuit, the airflow path, and the electrical control system each feed into the cooling output the evaporator coil produces. A failure in any one of these 3 subsystems produces the AC not cooling condition at the output stage.

AC not cooling requires [preventative maintenance](doc:preventative-maintenance) as the primary upstream defense. Scheduled maintenance catches low refrigerant pressure, restricted airflow, and early compressor wear before any of these conditions collapse the evaporator coil temperature differential below 15°F. Systems that receive annual preventative maintenance hold discharge air temperature within 2°F to 3°F of the thermostat set point across the full cooling season.

AC not cooling left unresolved causes compressor damage within one to two cooling seasons. The compressor runs continuously without completing a refrigeration cycle, which overheats the motor windings and causes permanent failure. Compressor replacement costs significantly more than refrigerant recharge or airflow correction, which makes early diagnosis the lowest-cost resolution path.
