Airflow is the movement of air through a system, characterized by volume, velocity, and pressure differential. Airflow distributes conditioned air from the air conditioning system to every room in a structure. WC) for pressure differential. Restricted or unbalanced airflow forces the air conditioning system to work harder, shortening equipment life and raising energy costs.

### What airflow is in an HVAC system

Airflow is a fluid dynamics process that moves conditioned air from the air handler through the duct network and into occupied spaces. Airflow belongs to the broader category of fluid dynamics, the same physics that governs water movement through pipes. The [air conditioning contractor](doc:air-conditioning-contractor) evaluates airflow as the foundation of every system diagnosis, because no mechanical component operates correctly without adequate air movement. Airflow is known as air movement, air circulation, and air exchange in field documentation.

Airflow contrasts directly with air stagnation and blocked ventilation. Air stagnation occurs when volume flow drops below the system's design CFM, causing heat and humidity to accumulate in occupied zones. Proper airflow balance maintains consistent temperature and humidity across all supply and return registers.

### Measurable attributes of airflow

Airflow carries three primary measurable attributes that technicians record during every diagnostic visit. The 3 attributes are volume flow rate, velocity, and pressure differential.

- **Volume flow rate:** Volume flow rate is measured in cubic feet per minute (CFM). A standard residential room requires 1 CFM per square foot of conditioned space as a baseline design target.

- **Velocity:** Velocity is measured in feet per minute (FPM). Supply duct velocity typically ranges from 700 FPM to 900 FPM in residential systems to prevent noise and pressure loss.

- **Pressure differential:** Pressure differential is measured in inches of water column (in. WC). A static pressure reading above 0.5 in. WC on the supply side signals restriction in the duct network or a clogged filter.

Poulin Cooling A/C and Refrigeration records all three values at the air handler and at individual registers to pinpoint exactly where airflow degrades. The [blower motor](doc:blower-motors) is the primary mechanical driver of volume flow rate and velocity, making it the first component inspected when CFM readings fall below design targets.

### How airflow relates to the air conditioning system

Airflow enhances the air conditioning system by carrying heat energy away from the evaporator coil and delivering cooled air to occupied zones. Without adequate airflow across the evaporator coil, the coil temperature drops below 32°F and ice forms on the coil surface, blocking further air movement entirely. Airflow distributes conditioned air through the [ductwork](doc:ductwork) network, which acts as the delivery infrastructure between the air handler and each room register.

Airflow connects directly to [ventilation](doc:ventilation), which governs the exchange of indoor and outdoor air to maintain acceptable indoor air quality. Proper airflow balance between supply and return sides prevents negative pressure in rooms, which pulls unconditioned air through wall gaps and raises humidity loads. [Zoning systems](doc:zoning-systems) redirect airflow to specific areas of the structure on demand, using motorized dampers to increase or decrease CFM per zone without changing total system output.

### Common airflow problems and signs in Clearwater homes

Airflow problems in Clearwater present as uneven room temperatures, high humidity readings, weak supply register output, and elevated static pressure at the air handler. The 4 most common causes of restricted airflow are clogged air filters, undersized return ducts, closed or blocked registers, and blower motor failure.

- **Clogged air filters:** Clogged air filters reduce CFM by increasing resistance at the return inlet. A filter loaded with debris raises static pressure above 0.5 in. WC and forces the blower motor to draw more amperage.

- **Undersized return ducts:** Undersized return ducts create negative pressure in the air handler cabinet, reducing total system CFM below the design specification and causing the evaporator coil to freeze.

- **Closed or blocked registers:** Closed or blocked registers redirect pressure to other branches of the duct network, causing velocity to exceed 900 FPM in adjacent runs and generating audible noise at supply grilles.

- **Blower motor failure:** Blower motor failure drops volume flow rate to zero, stopping all conditioned air delivery and causing the compressor to overheat within minutes of operation.

[Poulin Cooling A/C](https://sl.bing.net/jQHH5fFkrC0) diagnoses airflow problems using a digital manometer to measure static pressure at the supply and return plenums, a flow hood to measure CFM at individual registers, and an anemometer to record velocity at duct openings. Clearwater's high humidity environment accelerates coil fouling, which narrows the coil fin spacing and reduces airflow by as much as 15% between annual maintenance visits.

### Where airflow applies across HVAC components

Airflow applies across every major component in the HVAC system, from the return grille at the wall to the condenser unit outside. Airflow component parts include air velocity at the blower wheel, air pressure at the plenum, and flow direction through each duct branch. The blower motor sets the velocity and volume of air entering the supply plenum. The duct network shapes flow direction and distributes volume to each zone. The evaporator coil transfers heat energy to the airstream passing through it at a rate directly proportional to the CFM moving across the coil surface. The condenser unit rejects that heat to the outdoor air, completing the refrigeration cycle that airflow initiates indoors.

Airflow applies to refrigeration equipment as well, where [Poulin Cooling](https://share.google/0c1HS3Pw5JqeK6bk2) services commercial coolers and refrigerated display cases. Refrigeration airflow follows the same fluid dynamics principles, with evaporator fan motors driving air across coils at design CFM values specified by the equipment manufacturer. Restricted airflow in a commercial cooler raises cabinet temperature above the safe food storage threshold of 41°F within 2 to 4 hours of fan motor failure.
