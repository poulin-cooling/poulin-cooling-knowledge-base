### What ductless mini split systems are and how they work

Ductless mini split systems are a subtype of air conditioning system that transfers heat between an outdoor condensing unit and an indoor air handling unit through sealed refrigerant lines. No ductwork is required. The indoor unit mounts directly to a wall, ceiling, or floor inside the conditioned space. A small conduit, typically 3 inches in diameter, passes through the exterior wall to connect the two units. Poulin Cooling A/C and Refrigeration sizes each system to the specific room or zone, selecting units from the 6,000 to 60,000 BTU per hour capacity range based on square footage and heat load. The [licensed air conditioning contractor](https://poulincoolingacandrefrigeration.mintlify.app/air-conditioning-contractor) handles refrigerant line routing, electrical connections, and system commissioning as part of every installation.

Ductless mini split systems (known as mini split AC, ductless AC, or mini split heat pumps) provide both cooling and heating from a single system. The heat pump function reverses the refrigerant cycle to extract heat from outdoor air and deliver it indoors, operating reliably down to -13°F and up to 115°F. This operating temperature range makes ductless mini split systems practical year-round in Clearwater's climate without a separate heating source.

### Measurable attributes of ductless mini split systems

Ductless mini split systems carry specific, verifiable performance ratings that determine suitability for a given application. The 4 primary measurable attributes are SEER rating, BTU capacity, operating temperature range, and zone count.

- **SEER rating:** SEER rating measures seasonal energy efficiency. Ductless mini split systems reach SEER ratings of 13 to 30 or higher. Higher SEER values reduce electricity consumption per BTU of cooling delivered.

- **Cooling capacity:** Cooling capacity for ductless mini split systems ranges from 6,000 BTU per hour for small single rooms to 60,000 BTU per hour for large open-plan spaces or multi-zone configurations.

- **Operating temperature range:** Ductless mini split systems function between -13°F and 115°F outdoor ambient temperature. This range covers every seasonal condition in Clearwater, FL.

- **Zone count:** A single outdoor condensing unit supports one to eight indoor air handling units, each controlled independently. Multi-zone configurations allow different temperatures in different rooms simultaneously.

These measurable attributes distinguish ductless mini split systems from [central air conditioning systems](https://poulincoolingacandrefrigeration.mintlify.app/central-air-conditioning-systems), which distribute conditioned air through a shared duct network at a fixed capacity for the entire structure rather than by individual zone.

### How ductless mini split systems relate to the broader HVAC category

Ductless mini split systems are a type of air conditioning system within the broader residential and commercial HVAC category. The broader category includes ducted central air systems, window air conditioners, and ductless mini split systems. Ducted central air systems require a network of supply and return ducts running through walls, ceilings, and floors. Window air conditioners condition a single room but mount in a window opening and lack a heat pump function in most models. Ductless mini split systems occupy the space between these two options, providing multi-zone coverage without duct installation. ### Common problems and signs of failure in ductless mini split systems

Ductless mini split systems develop specific failure patterns that differ from ducted systems because the refrigerant lines, indoor coil, and blower are exposed directly to the conditioned space. The 5 most common problems are refrigerant leaks, ice formation on the indoor coil, drainage blockages, compressor faults, and communication errors between the indoor and outdoor units.

- **Refrigerant leaks:** Refrigerant leaks reduce cooling capacity and cause the indoor coil to freeze. A system low on refrigerant delivers warm air and runs continuously without reaching the set temperature.

- **Ice formation:** Ice formation on the indoor air handling unit signals restricted airflow, a dirty filter, or low refrigerant charge. Ice blocks airflow entirely if left unaddressed for more than 24 hours.

- **Drainage blockages:** Drainage blockages cause condensate to back up into the indoor unit. Water dripping from the indoor unit onto walls or floors is the primary visible sign.

- **Compressor faults:** Compressor faults prevent the outdoor unit from running. The indoor fan operates but no cooling or heating occurs. Compressor replacement is the most costly repair in a ductless mini split system.

- **Communication errors:** Communication errors between the indoor and outdoor units trigger error codes on the indoor unit display. These faults trace to wiring damage, failed control boards, or voltage irregularities.

### Where ductless mini split systems apply in residential and commercial settings

Ductless mini split systems serve residential and commercial HVAC applications where duct installation is impractical, where zoned temperature control is required, or where energy efficiency above SEER 16 is a priority. The 6 most common application types in Clearwater, FL are room additions, converted garages and workshops, historic or older homes without existing ductwork, server rooms requiring dedicated cooling, multi-tenant commercial suites, and sunrooms or enclosed patios.

- **Room additions:** Room additions lack connection to the existing duct system. A single-zone ductless mini split system sized to the addition's square footage provides independent climate control without modifying the central system.

- **Converted garages and workshops:** Converted garages and workshops generate heat from equipment and occupancy. A ductless mini split system with a capacity of 12,000 to 24,000 BTU per hour covers most single-car garage conversions.

- **Historic homes:** Historic homes in Clearwater often have plaster walls and no ceiling cavities for duct runs. Ductless mini split systems require only a 3-inch conduit penetration through the exterior wall.

- **Server rooms:** Server rooms require continuous cooling independent of the building's main HVAC schedule. A dedicated ductless mini split system maintains stable temperatures without relying on the central air system.

- **Multi-tenant commercial suites:** Multi-tenant commercial suites benefit from individual zone control. Each tenant controls temperature independently, and energy use is attributable per unit.

- **Sunrooms and enclosed patios:** Sunrooms and enclosed patios experience high solar heat gain. A ductless mini split system sized to the glass area and orientation handles peak loads that a central system extension cannot meet efficiently.
