Common HVAC problems homeowners face in Clearwater

Homeowners in Clearwater face HVAC problems driven by the region's high humidity, salt air exposure, and extended cooling seasons that place sustained demand on residential systems. The 3 most common problems homeowners report are refrigerant leaks, compressor failures, and thermostat malfunctions. Ignoring these problems leads to rising energy bills, accelerated equipment wear, and complete system failure during peak summer heat.

  • Refrigerant leaks: Refrigerant leaks reduce system cooling capacity and force the compressor to overwork, shortening its operational lifespan.
  • Compressor failures: Compressor failures halt the entire cooling cycle, leaving the home without conditioned air until the unit is repaired or replaced.
  • Thermostat malfunctions: Thermostat malfunctions cause erratic temperature cycling, uneven cooling across rooms, and unnecessary energy consumption.
  • Clogged air filters: Clogged air filters restrict airflow, reduce indoor air quality, and force the system to draw more power to maintain set temperatures.
  • Drainage line blockages: Drainage line blockages cause water overflow that damages ceilings, walls, and flooring in owner-occupied homes.
