### What NATE certification is

NATE certification is a professional credential issued by North American Technician Excellence that confirms an HVACR technician meets a defined standard of knowledge and skill. The full name is North American Technician Excellence Certification, referred to as the NATE credential or HVAC certification. NATE certification is a type of professional certification, placing it in the same category as other trade credentials that require demonstrated competency before issuance.

NATE certification validates the expertise of HVAC technicians through two core components: knowledge exams and a practical skills assessment. Technicians who earn the credential must complete continuing education units (CEUs) to maintain it. The credential contrasts directly with unlicensed or unqualified service, where no third-party body has confirmed the technician's ability to perform HVACR work correctly.

NATE certification connects directly to the broader framework of [HVAC licensing](hvac-licensing.md), which governs the legal authorization to perform HVACR work. Licensing establishes the legal floor; NATE certification establishes a measurable competency standard above that floor.

### Measurable attributes of NATE certification

NATE certification carries 3 primary measurable attributes that define its scope and requirements. The 3 attributes are validity period, minimum test score, and specialty areas covered.

- **Validity period:** Validity period for NATE certification is 2 years. After 2 years, the technician must renew through continuing education or re-examination to maintain the credential.

- **Minimum test score:** Minimum test score for the NATE certification exam is 70%. A technician who scores below 70% does not earn the credential and must retake the relevant knowledge exam.

- **Specialty areas covered:** Specialty areas covered by NATE certification reach up to 9 distinct HVACR disciplines. Each specialty exam tests domain-specific knowledge, so a technician certified in air conditioning service holds a different credential than one certified in heat pump installation.

These 3 measurable attributes make NATE certification one of the most objectively verifiable quality signals available when selecting an HVAC technician. ### What the NATE certification exam covers

NATE certification exam content covers both core knowledge and specialty-specific knowledge across up to 9 HVACR disciplines. The exam structure separates into a core knowledge section and a specialty section, so a technician must demonstrate general HVACR competency before earning credit in a specific area such as air conditioning service, heat pump installation, or commercial refrigeration.

The 3 component parts of the NATE certification process are knowledge exams, practical skills assessment, and continuing education units. Each component serves a distinct function in confirming technician readiness.

- **Knowledge exams:** Knowledge exams test the technician's understanding of HVACR theory, system components, and correct procedures for the chosen specialty area. A minimum score of 70% is required to pass.

- **Practical skills assessment:** Practical skills assessment confirms the technician performs hands-on tasks correctly, not only answers written questions accurately.

- **Continuing education units (CEUs):** Continuing education units maintain the credential after the initial 2-year validity period expires, ensuring the technician stays current with evolving HVACR standards and equipment.

### Signs a technician lacks NATE certification and why it matters

NATE certification absence is identifiable through 4 common signs that indicate a technician has not met the national competency standard. The 4 signs are inability to provide a credential number, expired certification beyond the 2-year window, no specialty designation matching the job type, and no record of CEU completion.

- **No credential number:** No credential number means the technician has not passed the NATE certification exam or the credential has lapsed. A valid NATE credential carries a verifiable identification number.

- **Expired certification:** Expired certification beyond the 2-year validity period means the technician has not renewed through CEUs or re-examination and no longer holds active NATE status.

- **Mismatched specialty:** Mismatched specialty means the technician holds a NATE credential in one discipline but performs work in a different specialty area, such as a heat pump specialist performing commercial refrigeration service.

- **No CEU record:** No CEU record at renewal time means the technician has not maintained the continuing education requirement and the credential renewal is invalid.
