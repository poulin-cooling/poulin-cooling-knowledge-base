### Common building code violations in HVAC installations and their consequences

Building code violations in HVAC installations fall into predictable categories that inspectors flag repeatedly in Clearwater. Building code violations do not resolve themselves. Each violation requires corrective work, a re-inspection fee, and added project time before the system receives approval.

The 4 most common HVAC-related building code violations in Clearwater are unpermitted equipment swaps, undersized electrical disconnects, improper condensate drainage, and missing refrigerant line insulation. Each violation is detectable during a standard mechanical inspection.

- **Unpermitted equipment swaps:** Unpermitted equipment swaps occur when a homeowner or contractor replaces a condensing unit or air handler without pulling a permit. Discovery during a home sale triggers mandatory retroactive permitting and inspection.

- **Undersized electrical disconnects:** Undersized electrical disconnects fail to meet the safety standards section of the Florida Building Code. The disconnect must match the minimum circuit ampacity listed on the equipment nameplate.

- **Improper condensate drainage:** Improper condensate drainage violates installation specifications requiring a primary drain, a secondary drain or overflow shutoff, and proper slope to an approved discharge point.

- **Missing refrigerant line insulation:** Missing refrigerant line insulation violates energy code requirements embedded within the Florida Building Code, reducing system efficiency and failing the energy compliance portion of the inspection.
